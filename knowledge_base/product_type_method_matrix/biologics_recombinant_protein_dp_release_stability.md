# Biologics / Recombinant Protein DP Release & Stability — Initial Method Matrix

## Scope

Initial MVP entry for recombinant protein drug product release and stability analytical planning.

This is a starting matrix and requires analytical SME, QA, and RA review before use in formal CMC work.

## Product Context

| Field | Initial Scope |
|---|---|
| Product type | Biologic / recombinant protein |
| Sample type | Drug product |
| Use case | Release and stability |
| Lifecycle stage | Development / registration / lifecycle support |

## Initial Analytical Categories

| Analytical Category | Candidate Method Types | Priority | Rationale | Review Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Identity | Peptide mapping, immunoassay, electrophoretic method, LC-MS where applicable | Required / conditional | Confirm product identity and distinguish from related proteins or excipients | Product-specific method selection required |
| Assay / content | RP-HPLC, UV, ELISA, LC-based assay, other suitable quantitative method | Required | Quantify active ingredient or relevant protein content | Must align with reportable result and specification |
| Potency | Cell-based bioassay, binding assay, reporter gene assay, mechanism-relevant assay | Required where biological activity is CQA | Demonstrate biological activity relevant to intended function | Requires careful control strategy and reference standard |
| Purity / related proteins | SEC-HPLC, CE-SDS, SDS-PAGE, RP-HPLC, icIEF | Required / conditional | Monitor aggregates, fragments, charge variants, or related impurities | Method depends on product degradation pathway |
| Degradation / stability-indicating | Stability-indicating LC method, bioassay, orthogonal methods | Required for stability strategy | Support stability assessment and shelf-life | Forced degradation rationale may be needed |
| Process-related impurities | HCP, residual DNA, residual host-cell or process impurities | Conditional | Required where relevant to process and product risk | May be DS- or DP-specific |
| Microbiological quality | Microbial limits, sterility where applicable, endotoxin where applicable | Required / conditional | Support product safety and compendial / regulatory expectations | Dosage form and route dependent |
| Appearance / physicochemical | Appearance, pH, osmolality, water content, dissolution/disintegration where applicable | Conditional | Support dosage form and formulation quality | Depends on DP format |
| Content uniformity / dosage unit uniformity | Compendial or validated quantitative assay | Conditional | Required for solid dosage forms where applicable | Must be aligned with dosage form and specification |
